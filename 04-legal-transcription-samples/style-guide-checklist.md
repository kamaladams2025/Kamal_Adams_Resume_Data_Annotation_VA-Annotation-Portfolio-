# Style-Guide Checklist — Verbatim Legal Transcription

All dialogue below is **invented for demonstration purposes** — no real case, client, or deponent.

## Formatting rules demonstrated

| Rule | Applied? | Where |
|---|---|---|
| Full verbatim retention (false starts, filler words not cleaned up) | ✅ | Line 3 ("I — I don't, uh, recall exactly") |
| Speaker labels in full caps, consistent across the document | ✅ | Q. / A. / MR. [SURNAME]: |
| Crosstalk/interruption notation | ✅ | Line 6 |
| Inaudible-audio marker with timestamp | ✅ | Line 8 |
| Non-verbal sound notation in brackets | ✅ | Line 9 |
| Numbers under a hundred spelled out per house style; larger figures rendered as numerals | ✅ | Line 5, Line 10 |
| Exhibit/document references formatted per citation convention | ✅ | Line 11 |

## Synthetic excerpt

```
1   Q.    Can you state your full name for the record, please.

2   A.    Yes. Jordan Avery Mitchell.

3   A.    I — I don't, uh, recall exactly what time the meeting
          started, but it was sometime in the afternoon.

4   Q.    Approximately what time would you estimate?

5   A.    I'd say around two o'clock, maybe two-thirty.

6   MR. HENDERSON: Objection — calls for speculation.

7   Q.    You can answer if you have an estimate.

8   A.    [INAUDIBLE 00:14:22] — I think it was closer to three.

9   A.    [CLEARS THROAT] Sorry. Closer to three, I believe.

10  Q.    And were there approximately fifteen people in the room,
          or more?

11  A.    Referring to Exhibit 4, the sign-in sheet lists
          fourteen attendees.
```

## Notes
- Line 3 preserves the speaker's stammer and filler word exactly as spoken — verbatim style does not smooth out hesitation, since the transcript is a legal record of exactly what was said.
- Line 6 shows an attorney interjection formatted as its own speaker turn rather than folded into the witness's answer, preserving the actual sequence of speech for the record.
- Line 8's timestamp marker follows HH:MM:SS convention so an editor or reviewer can locate the exact point of inaudible audio in the source recording.
